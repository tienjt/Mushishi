List of Named Mushi Species
Name Episode Appearance
Ah "Tender Horns" (#3)
  • Ah are snail-shaped mushi that infect the ears of humans, causing all sound to be blocked.
  • They co-exist with the mushi Un and feast on the silence that the Un create.
  • Victims of the Ah and Un will grow horns on their head that capture the echo of every mushi sound on Earth, which is like a constant tidal wave of sound.
Ganpuku "Fortune Eye and Misfortune Eye" (#25)
  • Ganpuku are rare mushi that enter the body of any living creature that looks at it directly and take control of the eyes.
  • Over time, the host develops the ability to see unnatural distances, through objects, and eventually the past and future of anyone she looks at.
  • When a Ganpuku finally completes its takeover of the host's eyes, the eyes fall out and the mushi breaks free.
Imeno no Awai "Pillow Lane" (#4)
  • Imeno no Awai are flying mushi that take refuge in people's pillows and infest the dreams of their hosts.
  • They may cause their host's dreams to become reality, which is often mistaken as proof of prophetic ability.
Kagebi "The Journey to the Field of Fire" (#24)
  • The life cycle of Kagebi has two known stages: the larval form, which resembles a blue weed and is highly invasive, and the adult form, called "ghost fire."
  • If the larval form is burned, the adult form will manifest and feast on the body heat of living organisms.
  • Anyone eating food cooked by Kagebi will slowly freeze, as will the person unlucky enough to inhale it.
Kumohami "The White Which Lives Within the Ink Stone" (#10)
  • Kumohami resemble cumulus clouds and feed on moisture in the atmosphere, converting it to snow and hail.
  • They rely on the wind to move; if there is no wind for an extended period of time, they will fall to the ground in the form of precipitation and hibernate, reviving themselves only if contacted by water.
Magari-dake "Inside the Cage" (#14)
  • The Magari-dake takes the form of a white bamboo tree and attaches itself to the established root system of an existing bamboo forest, leeching away water and nutrients.
  • If human females come in close contact with the Magari-dake, the mushi may impregnate them. Children born from an infected mother are half-mushi and rely on water from the original Magari-dake to survive as they cannot consume any other food or liquid.
Mugura "The Sleeping Mountain" (#11)
  • Mugura live underground and act as a mountain's nervous system.
  • A skilled mushishi like Ginko can send their consciousness into the Mugura to obtain information about the mountain.
  • In this episode, Ginko utilizes the Mugura to locate a mushishi who is lost somewhere on the mountain.
Suiko "The Traveling Swamp" (#5)
  • Suiko are elusive, liquid mushi that that can be found in old water veins, ponds, and wells.
  • The person who inadventently drinks water containing Suiko will develop an unbearable thirst and consume more and more water until his or her flesh liquifies and dissolves.
Un "Tender Horns" (#3)
  • Un are snail-shaped mushi that infect the ears of humans, devouring all sound before it reaches the ears.
  • They co-exist with the mushi Ah and provide the silence that the Ah feast on.
  • Victims of the Un and Ah will grow horns on their head that capture the echo of every mushi sound on Earth, which is like a constant tidal wave of sound.
Yasabi "The Sound of Rust" (#23)
  • Yasabi are a rust-like mushi that assist in the decomposition process of dead organisms, emitting a specific sound while doing so to alert other Yasabi of a potential meal.
  • If no corpses are to be found, they will attach themselves to living beings instead, causing arthritic symptoms.
