List of Mushishi Episodes
# Title Air Date
1 "The Green Seat" October 23, 2005
Ginko investigates the case of a boy whose drawings, if drawn by his left hand, come to life.
2 "The Light of the Eyelid" October 30, 2005
Ginko gives aid to a little girl who spends her days locked in a warehouse because she can't bear sunlight.
3 "Tender Horns" November 6, 2005
A boy who somehow has horns growing on his head is fated to die like his mother, who suffered of the same problem, unless Ginko is able to figure out a cure.
4 "The Pillow Pathway" November 13, 2005
Ginko investigates the case of a boy whose drawings, if drawn by his left hand, come to life.
5 "The Traveling Swamp" November 20, 2005
Ginko investigates the case of a mysterious girl who is seen in a swamp which moves by itself.
6 "Those Who Inhale the Dew" November 27, 2005
Ginko's services are requested by a boy who lives on a distant island to investigate the case of a girl revered by the people there as a god.
7 "Raindrops and Rainbows" December 4, 2005
Ginko encounters a man who has a strange habit of pursuing rainbows.
8 "Where Sea Meets Man" December 11, 2005
While traveling along a beach, Ginko meets a man who hopes to find his long-lost wife.
9 "The Heavy Seed" December 18, 2005
Ginko arrives at a village where there are unusual harvests, always occurring after natural disasters, and always involving the death of one of its inhabitants.
10 "The White Which Lives Within the Ink Stone" January 8, 2006
A group of kids get themselves in trouble when they mess with one of the mushi-related items from Adashino's collection.
11 "The Sleeping Mountain" January 8, 2006
During his travels, Ginko meets an old mushishi who tells him the story of how he became the guardian of the mountain where he lives, and why he can't leave there for the remainder of his life.
12 "One-Eyed Fish" January 15, 2006
An orphan is adopted by Nui, a white-haired female Mushishi. Ginko's origin is revealed.
13 "One-Night Bridge" January 22, 2006
Ginko arrives in a village where lives a girl who fell from a bridge three years before. Though she apparently survived the fall, she is not the person she used to be anymore, to the despair of the young man who loves her.
14 "Inside the Cage" January 29, 2006
While traveling through a bamboo forest, Ginko encounters a man who somehow can't leave the forest, no matter how he tries. Curiously, his wife and child also share the same issue.
15 "Pretense of Spring" February 5, 2006
Ginko gets shelter in a cabin during the winter. The boy who lives there claims there is a place where spring blooms early and every time he goes there he ends up sleeping for days.
16 "Sunrise Serpent" February 12, 2006
Ginko helps a boy who is worried about his mother because she is little by little forgetting her memories.
17 "Pickers of Empty Cocoons" February 19, 2006
Ginko is worried about the girl who handles the cocoons used as mailboxes by the Mushishi, as she is still searching for her sister who disappeared when they were children.
18 "Clothes That Embrace the Mountain" February 26, 2006
Ginko gets his hands on a coat apparently possessed by a mushi. Hoping to figure out the truth, he starts looking for the coat's first owner.
19 "String from the Sky" March 5, 2006
Ginko helps a girl who has been missing since she grabbed a string she saw hanging from the sky. After she returns home, strange things start to happen to her.
20 "A Sea of Writings" March 12, 2006
Ginko comes to a house which has a library full of mushi-related scrolls. There, he meets the girl who writes the scrolls, and hears the story of the curse that has been afflicting her family for generations.
21 "Cotton Changeling" May 15, 2006
Ginko is called to see a child who was developing a green rash through his body. He quickly figures that the child and his four brothers are not human.
22 "Shrine in the Sea" May 22, 2006
Ginko investigates a village with a shrine rumored to give people another life.
23 "The Sound of Rust" May 29, 2006
A girl is ostracized by her village because, according to a rumor, she is the cause of a strange rust that afflicts the houses and people there. Now it's up to Ginko to figure out the truth.
24 "The Journey to the Field of Fire" June 5, 2006
Trying to dispose an unknown type of mushi which threatens her village, the local mushishi decides to burn down the whole mountain where it lives. But Ginko does not agree with the idea.
25 "Eye of Fortune, Eye of Misfortune" June 12, 2006
Ginko encounters a woman who was blind when she was a child, until a mushi entered her eyes and granted her the ability of sight. Although her sight improved day by day, she began seeing more than she wished for.
26 "The Sound of Footsteps on the Grass" June 19, 2006
When Ginko was a child, he lived for a while with the Watari, a group of nomads, which every year visited a mysterious mountain. Among the nomads, there is another boy, Isaza, who befriends the son of the family who owns the mountain, and their friendship grows despite them barely seeing each other.
