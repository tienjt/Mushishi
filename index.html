About


Mushishi (Japanese: 蟲師) is an animated television series by Studio Artland that aired on Fuji Television from October 2005 to June 2006. It is an adaptation of the manga series of the same name by Yuki Urushibara. Funimation licensed its release in North America, while Madman Entertainment and Revelation Films licensed it for Australia and the United Kingdom respectively. Marvelous Entertainment and Avex released the series from January 25 to September 27, 2006 in five DVDs for sale, and at the same time in nine DVDs for rental. On March 28, 2008 a DVD box set containing all episodes was released; it was followed by a Blu-ray box set on March 27, 2009, and a Limited Edition Blu-ray box on December 20, 2013.


A sequel, which aired from April to December 2014, has been licensed in North America by Aniplex of America. It adapted the remaining chapters of the manga that had not been adapted in the original television series. Additionally, two television specials aired in 2014 and a film was released in 2015.



Plot


Mushishi is set in an imaginary time between the Edo and Meiji periods. The story features primitive, ubiquitous creatures called mushi that often display what seem to be supernatural powers. Due to their ethereal nature most humans are incapable of perceiving mushi and are oblivious to their existence, but there are a few who possess the ability to see and interact with mushi. One such person is Ginko, the main character of the series. He employs himself as a mushi master (mushishi), traveling from place to place to research them and aid people suffering from problems caused by them.


The series is an episodic anthology in which the only common elements among episodes are Ginko and the various types of mushi. A majority of the stories do not focus on Ginko; rather they rely on him as a catalyst to move the story forward by diagnosing or curing mushi-related illnesses and phenomena.
